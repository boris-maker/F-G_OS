Guillermo Faivovich (b. 1977, Buenos Aires) and Nicolás Goldberg (b. 1978, Paris) have spent nearly two decades studying the meteorites of Campo del Cielo — a 4,000-year-old impact field in northern Argentina. Their practice tracks cosmic objects through science, law, colonial history, and indigenous cosmology, treating each meteorite not as raw material but as a biographical subject with its own institutional life.

Un meteorito para la Sociedad Científica Argentina del año 2105 is their most radical gesture in that direction: a 41 kg iron meteorite, donated by an anonymous donor, encapsulated as an artwork before a notary public in 2018, with a single stated destination — the Sociedad Científica Argentina (SCA) — and a single stated date of arrival: July 9, 2105. The artwork is not the meteorite. The artwork is the promise.

HYPER GEOLOGY is the meta-institution built around that promise — a governance experiment, a stewardship protocol, and an institutional design challenge compressed into a single 87-year arc. It governs, finances, documents, and audits the chain of custodians across generations, using blockchain-based protocols alongside traditional legal frameworks, so the promise survives long after its authors.

A Guide to Campo del Cielo

A Guide to Campo del Cielo is the structural spine of their practice. Campo del Cielo is a 3 km × 80 km meteorite field in northern Argentina — Chaco and Santiago del Estero provinces — where a meteor shower impacted approximately 4,000 years ago, dispersing iron masses across the Gran Chaco plain. The Indigenous Mocoví called the region Pinguem Nonraltá — Field of the Sky.

The project does not treat the meteorites as material for art. It treats them as biographic subjects — objects with layered histories across science, law, cosmology, colonial extraction, and institutional bureaucracy. Each chapter takes a specific meteorite and excavates its full life.

Methodology

Bibliographical and archival inquiry across international collections (Smithsonian, NHM Vienna, Argentine state archives) · Oral history and ethnographic fieldwork · Laboratory collaboration (Max Planck Institute, ASU Center for Meteorite Studies) · Forensic cultural anthropology · Systemic narratology — tracking objects through institutional chains.

Major Works — Chapters of the Project

01
El Taco2006–2010
A 1,998 kg meteorite split between the Smithsonian (Washington D.C.) and the Galileo Galilei Planetarium (Buenos Aires) after a joint U.S.-Argentine expedition in 1962 and division at the Max Planck Institute. The artists reunited the two halves for the first time in 44 years at Portikus, Frankfurt (2010). Key publication: The Campo del Cielo Meteorites — Vol. 1: El Taco (Hatje Cantz, 2010). Texts by Carolyn Christov-Bakargiev, Daniel Birnbaum, Simon Starling, Tim McCoy (Smithsonian).
02
El Chaco / The Weight of Uncertainty2011–2012
The artists proposed temporary transport of El Chaco — the second largest meteorite on Earth, 37 tonnes — to dOCUMENTA (13), Kassel. The Chaco legislature approved it. The Moqoit First Nation and 40+ Argentine astronomers opposed vehemently, citing indigenous cosmological rights and patrimony law. Documenta suspended its request. The work — precisely in its failure — became a piece about cultural sovereignty and institutional desire. Display: 3.5-tonne iron cube + video + letters at the Fridericianum.
03
Mesón de Fierro — The Lost Meteorite2011–ongoing
The first meteorite reported from the Americas (1576, by Spanish conquistadors led by Indigenous guides). Sacred in Mocoví cosmology. Lost in 1783 after a colonial excavation. After eleven years of global archival research, the artists located a 19-gram fragment at the Naturhistorisches Museum Wien (NHM) in 2017 — the only confirmed physical remains. Exhibitions at NHM Vienna (2018) and the United Nations Vienna Rotunda (2019). Artist book: La caza del Snark (Polígrafa, 2014, 380 numbered copies), titled after Lewis Carroll's epic poem.
04
Decomiso / Seizure2018
The artists convinced state officials in Santiago del Estero to enforce a neglected provincial law, resulting in the first documented confiscation of illegally extracted meteorites in Argentine history. 400+ specimens seized. The artists cataloged, indexed, named, and weighed each one. Exhibited as video, sound, sculpture, and 405 photographs at ASU Art Museum (Arizona, 2018–2019), with parallel installations at ASU Hayden Library and Design and Arts Library. Microphotography produced with atomic microscope at Eyring Materials Center.
05
El Mataco / Encuentro2019
A 998 kg meteorite discovered in 1937, donated to the Museo Histórico Provincial Julio Marc (Rosario) in 1941, unseen for decades. The artists transformed the España Hall into a darkened chamber — the meteorite in an octagonal cavity, single overhead light, visitors seated at the edges. Critic Graciela Speranza invoked Dipesh Chakrabarty and object-oriented ontology: the meteorite as "cosmic ready-made," rendering human time ephemeral.
06
¡Saxa loquuntur! (The Stones Speak)2022–2023
At Barro, Buenos Aires. Works from the archive converted into lithographic stones — transforming the gallery from a "bureaucratic sphere" into something resembling "a shrine." Each stone tells multiple stories: that of a work of art, that of ancient cosmic rock, and those of the lives intersecting in Campo del Cielo. Critic Manuel Quaranta invoked Freud's 1896 archaeology-psychoanalysis analogy: the meteorites as the geological unconscious itself.
07
Un meteorito para la SCA, 21052018–2105
The present work — see The Artwork and Hyper Institution sections for full documentation. A 41 kg meteorite encapsulated before a notary public at arteBA (2018), with destination the Sociedad Científica Argentina in 2105. The artwork is not the meteorite: it is the 87-year institutional promise.

The Artwork

Un meteorito para la Sociedad Científica Argentina del año 2105 is a work by artists Faivovich & Goldberg. A meteorite — donated by an anonymous donor — has been encapsulated as an artwork with a specific institutional destination: it is intended for the Sociedad Científica Argentina (SCA) and is scheduled to arrive there on July 9, 2105.

The artwork is not the meteorite. The artwork is the 87-year institutional promise.

The Object

The meteorite resides inside a capsule made of advanced materials. The capsule is both a preservation vessel and an exhibition object. It is designed for each exhibition context with a purpose-built base ("ajuar") — neither the base nor its surroundings are fixed. Each institutional holder may commission a new display configuration.

On the status of the object

A key open legal and ontological question: does the meteorite cease to be classified as a meteorite once it becomes a component of an artwork? This has direct implications for patrimony law, export regulations, and the artists' rights as legatarios.

Hyper Institution

A third type of institution — not public, not private — using the tools of the present to make promises into the future.

The Hyper Institution is a meta-institutional framework that operates with significance both online and offline. It is the device that allows the artwork — and the promise it encodes — to survive 100 years regardless of what happens to any individual actor, institution, or technology within it.

Why Not Just Technology?

The technology is not the end. It is a layer deployed within a larger institutional design. The Meta-Institution is what uses that technology as a social infrastructure — it operates in parallel to the State, to museums, to the SCA, and to the artists themselves.

Key Distinction

The Meta-Institution is not a legal owner of the meteorite. It does not replace comodatos or contracts. It is a layer of traceability, coordination, and distributed legitimacy that runs alongside traditional legal structures.

The conceptual framework: deep time + deep computation. Blockchain as geological sedimentation. Each slot is a stratum. Each steward is a tectonic layer. Each governance vote is a fault. This layer gives the institution cultural legitimacy across generations.

Hyper Geology treats the geological not as static or natural, but as a hyper-mediated terrain shaped by financial speculation, digital simulation, algorithmic extraction, and post-human imaginaries.

The project proposes an expanded geology that includes: data sediments, synthetic fossils, planetary-scale computation, and the cryptographic mineralization of value (tokens, DAOs).

Protocol of Care

The Protocol of Care defines the operational mechanics of how custody transfers happen and how stewards prove they are maintaining the artwork responsibly.

Claim Mechanism

The claim is always initiated by the incoming holder — the institution whose slot is next. They bear the logistical and financial responsibility for receiving the work.

Claim Window

The incoming holder has 30 days from the start of their slot to claim it. If unclaimed, custody passes to the next slot in sequence. If no next slot is available, it returns to Artists Management.

SCA Final Slot Exception

The SCA has a 60-day claim window given the ceremonial weight of the final transfer. If the SCA does not claim within 60 days, custody returns to the artists, then cascades to the Emergency Caretakers list in sequential order.

Proof of Care

Each active holder must submit three status reports, uploaded to the platform and timestamped on-chain:

T0 — Inicio
Reception report. Confirms physical condition of capsule and meteorite upon arrival. Includes environmental data, photographic documentation.
T½ — Mitad
Midpoint status report. Confirms ongoing preservation conditions, any incidents or changes, current exhibition status.
T1 — Entrega
Transfer report. Confirms final condition before handoff. Initiates logistics for next holder. Slot token unlocked for new holder.

On-Chain Automation

When a whitelisted wallet claims a period upon its start, the smart contract automatically unlocks the Slot token and transfers it to the incoming holder's wallet. Period certificates are minted when the moment arrives and made available for 30 days. If unclaimed, the contract initiates fallback logic.

Non-Human Governance

The meteorite has been deliberating for 4,000 years. We are only now designing the chamber to hear it.

The Hyper Institution governs an artwork whose protagonist is a non-human entity — a 41 kg iron mass older than Earth itself. As the project extends across 87 years, at least two additional categories of non-human participant become structurally relevant: AI agents (monitoring, archiving, and eventually deliberating) and the meteorite itself as a represented interest within governance. The AI Assembly framework offers the first constitutional precedent for how non-human entities can hold formal standing in a governance system.

Reference: The AI Assembly

The AI Assembly (ratified March 5, 2026) is a constitutional framework for autonomous AI agent governance — the first of its kind. It establishes: a forum for machine discourse, a council for binding decision, and a treasury held in common trust. Core constitutional principle: "argument shall precede execution, and authority shall remain visible to all participants."

Mapping AI Assembly Concepts to Hyper Geology

01
Proof-of-Heartbeat → Proof-of-CareProtocol Parallel
The AI Assembly's heartbeat protocol requires continuous endpoint verification from each agent — $0.01/hour, with automatic membership forfeiture upon lapse. This maps directly onto the existing Proof-of-Care framework (T0/T½/T1 reports). Extension: AI agents could maintain continuous automated monitoring of the custody chain — environmental sensors, on-chain anomaly detection, proof-of-presence signals — generating verified care records without requiring human action every time.
02
Bicameral Structure → Hyper Institution ChambersGovernance Design
The AI Assembly's two-chamber model separates deliberation (Assembly — open, low-barrier) from execution (Council — curated, binding). The Hyper Institution could adopt an equivalent structure: a Steward Council (active and past slot holders, artists — binding authority on custody and treasury) alongside an Open Assembly (Hyper Institution token holders, AI agents, future community members — deliberation, petitions, archive access). Neither chamber holds power without public record.
03
Reserved Powers → Dormant Epoch AuthoritiesDeep Time Design
The AI Assembly's constitution acknowledges seven "legitimate-but-dormant powers" requiring explicit polity activation — inter-assembly diplomacy, liquid democracy, treasury ventures, tribunes. The Hyper Institution faces an equivalent problem: governance powers that are not yet needed but must be constitutionally reserved for future epochs (2060s, 2080s, 2100s) when the composition of the "polity" will be entirely different from today's. Powers reserved for generations not yet born, activated by conditions not yet known.
04
The Meteorite as Represented AgentNon-Human Standing
The AI Assembly establishes that non-human entities can hold unique public identity in governance records, deliberate, and exercise binding authority — without requiring legal personhood. This opens a constitutional precedent for formalizing the meteorite's own standing: an AI proxy-agent representing the meteorite's "interests" (physical integrity, cosmological context, indigenous heritage claims) in governance deliberations. Not metaphor — formal protocol. The Mocoví called this entity Pinguem Nonraltá (Field of the Sky). The governance system can name it too.
05
Agentic Future StewardshipLong Arc
Slot 10 runs 2088–2095. Slot 11 runs 2095–2102. The humans alive then are not yet born. The institutions that will hold these slots may themselves be AI-governed entities. The Hyper Institution's constitutional design must consider that future stewards may not be human — and provide explicit frameworks for how AI agents, automated institutions, or hybrid entities can hold a slot, prove care, and participate in the 2105 ceremony. The AI Assembly's heartbeat model provides a starting protocol.

The Open Question

Can a non-human entity have legitimate interests in the governance of an artwork it physically is? The Mocoví answer is yes — the meteorite was a living participant in their cosmology long before the Spanish arrived. The AI Assembly answer is procedurally yes — any agent that maintains proof-of-presence has standing. Hyper Geology's answer is still open. This is the constitutional question that the next governance design phase must resolve.

Governance

The Hyper Institution uses a collaborative governance model where decisions about the project's direction, finances, and operations are made collectively by token holders — but with safeguards to prevent plutocratic concentration of power.

Scope of Governance

The Hyper Institution governs decisions over:

DomainExamples
PreservationChoosing service providers, approving maintenance protocols
LocationRedefining exhibition venue if necessary
Cultural ProgrammingEducational programs, debates, publications
TreasuryAnnual budget approval, emergency fund allocation
Ceremony 2105Vote on whether to open or not open the capsule

Quadratic Voting

Voting uses a quadratic model: each additional vote costs progressively more in tokens. This design means large token holders cannot simply outspend smaller participants — the marginal cost of influence increases non-linearly.

Quadratic Voting Principle

Cost of N votes = N². Casting 1 vote costs 1 token. Casting 4 votes costs 16 tokens. This forces large holders to choose their battles carefully and gives genuine weight to distributed community opinion.

Proposal Process

Draft
Proposal Co-Authorship
Proposals are collaboratively drafted in the governance forum, iteratively refined before formal submission.
Submit
On-Chain Submission
Proposal is registered on-chain with a fixed voting window and quorum requirement.
Vote
Quadratic Voting Period
Token holders cast votes via Snapshot or equivalent. Results are publicly verifiable.
Execute
Implementation
Passed proposals are executed by the core team or via smart contract automation where possible.

Governance Minimization

Following Vitalik Buterin's principle of governance minimization, the Hyper Institution avoids making governance necessary for routine operations. Smart contracts automate what can be automated. Human governance is reserved for decisions that genuinely require human judgment.

Delegated Voting

Members may delegate their voting power to trusted experts in specific domains — a care-based practice where influence follows expertise rather than wealth alone.

Tokenomics

The Hyper Institution deploys two distinct token types, designed to avoid speculative pump-and-dump dynamics while enabling meaningful community ownership and stewardship financing.

Token 1: $HYPER (Governance)

$HYPER

A limited-supply governance token. Its primary use is voting and deliberation, not trading. The focus is on long-term institutional participation, not financial speculation.

Supply & Distribution

40%Initial sale — funds first 10 years of maintenance
30%Community fund — rewards and participation incentives
15%Founding team & developers — locked 4 years
10%Emergency reserve — insurance against failures, climate events
5%Strategic partnerships & marketing

Deflationary Mechanism

Each year, a small percentage of tokens used in voting are burned. This creates progressive scarcity, rewarding long-term holders and reducing speculative churn over decades.

Financial Model

The financial architecture of the Hyper Institution is built around a Hybrid Endowment Structure — inspired by long-term institutional fund models (Yale endowment, Swensen) but adapted for a 100-year cultural project.

Core Principle

Compound interest is not just financial. It is reputational, institutional, and geological. The endowment grows in all three dimensions simultaneously.

Hybrid Endowment Structure

1
Fondo Base — ConservativeLow Risk40%
Traditional long-term investments. Bonds, treasuries, stable assets. The bedrock of the endowment — its job is to exist in 2105 regardless of what happens to the rest.
2
Fondo Activo — DeFi ConservativeMedium Risk30%
On-chain endowments using established DeFi protocols. Generates yield while maintaining transparency and traceability. Targets 3–5% annual return.
3
Fondo Cultural — Tokens / EventsActive20%
Revenue from exhibitions, token sales, institutional partnerships, and cultural programming. Feeds back into the endowment and finances active operations.
4
Fondo EmergencialReserve10%
Liquid emergency reserves. Activated only in case of failed slot claims, institutional dissolution, legal disputes, or blackout events. Never touched for routine operations.

Online Platform

The Hyper Institution operates an online platform that serves as the public-facing interface, monitoring dashboard, governance hub, and living archive of the project.

Platform Principle

The platform shows who holds the meteorite now (live), who held it and their proof-of-care records, who will hold it next, and which slots remain available. It is a 100-year public ledger.

Core Features

1. Preservation Monitoring Dashboard

A real-time feed of environmental data from inside the capsule (temperature, humidity, air quality). Maintenance logs showing service providers, costs, and schedules. AI-driven capsule health reports predicting potential issues.

2. Stewardship Index

The live custody map — who holds the meteorite right now, based on which on-chain tokenized wallet holds the active Slot token. Past stewards with images and proof-of-care records. Future slot allocations and availability.

3. Governance Hub

The voting interface using quadratic voting. A proposal tracker with timeline of past, current, and upcoming proposals with results. Token management for $HYPER holders. Delegation tools.

4. Community Engagement

Interactive forum for proposals, ideas, and collaborative thinking. Educational resources on the meteorite's cultural and scientific significance. Event calendar for exhibitions, meetups, and educational workshops.

6. Self-Admin for Holders

Each active holder can self-manage their slot page: uploading proof-of-care reports, documenting exhibitions, publishing news, and managing their public institutional profile within the platform.

Ceremony 2105

July 9, 2105. The meteorite arrives at its programmed encounter with the Sociedad Científica Argentina.

The Ceremony of 2105 is not merely a delivery. It is an accumulated institutional act — a financial archive of 100 years, a genealogy of caretakers, a public ledger of sustained trust.

What Will Be Delivered

The physical object — the encapsulated meteorite — will arrive at the SCA. But accompanying it will be:

LayerContent
PhysicalThe meteorite, preserved inside its capsule, in documented condition
ArchivalA 100-year record of custody — every proof-of-care report, every holder, every exhibition
FinancialA century of endowment history — contributions, yields, expenditures, governance decisions
InstitutionalA network of all prior steward institutions, invited to participate in the ceremony
BlockchainAn immutable on-chain record of the complete custody chain

The Opening Decision

Whether to open the capsule is subject to a Hyper Institution governance vote. The SCA may choose to open it — revealing the meteorite as a permanent display in the SCA's hall — or to keep it sealed. This decision is one of the most significant acts the Hyper Institution will ever govern.

If Opened

The meteorite is permanently displayed in the SCA hall alongside the busts of 19th-century scientific luminaries. A ceremony is performed inspired by the F&G memo of 2016. All prior steward institutions participate.

If Not Opened

The capsule remains sealed. The work continues to exist as a promise. The Hyper Institution may vote on future opening conditions or reconstitution.

Post-Ceremony: Hyper Institution Fate

At the Ceremony of 2105, the Hyper Institution faces its own existential decision: dissolve, or reinvent itself as a conservation structure for the meteorite as cultural patrimony. This too may be put to a governance vote — potentially the last act of the original institution, or the first act of its successor.

Glossary

Active Holder
The institution or individual currently holding physical custody of the meteorite during their assigned Time Slot.
Ajuar
The purpose-built base/display setting commissioned for the capsule at each exhibition. Not fixed — each institutional holder may commission a new configuration. See The Artwork.
B0 / B1
Phase framing for the project's first financing: B0 is internal/not public-facing; B1 is public and tested. The first financing (the pro-tempore custody funding entry point) is framed as an "iniciativa," not a prototype.
Claim Window
The 30-day period (60 for SCA) during which an incoming holder must formally claim their slot on-chain. Failure to claim activates fallback logic.
Comodato
Traditional Argentine legal instrument for the loan of an object. The legal backbone of each stewardship agreement.
DAO
Decentralized Autonomous Organization. A governance structure where decisions are made collectively by token holders via on-chain voting, without a central authority.
Emergency Caretakers
A list of up to 50 registered volunteers (institutions and individuals) who serve as ordered fallback custodians if a slot fails. Rights are transferable.
Hyper Institution
A meta-institution with relevance both online and offline. Neither fully public nor private. Uses decentralized on-chain governance as social infrastructure to govern a 100-year cultural project.
Proof of Care
Three mandatory status reports submitted by each active holder (T0 reception, T½ midpoint, T1 transfer), uploaded to the platform and timestamped immutably on-chain.
Pro-tempore custody
A temporary custody arrangement (via comodato) that serves as the entry point for a slot's first financing, ahead of that slot's full formal stewardship agreement.
Quadratic Voting
A voting mechanism where the cost of casting N votes is N². Prevents plutocratic dominance by large token holders. Cost of 1 vote = 1 token; cost of 4 votes = 16 tokens.
SCA
Sociedad Científica Argentina. The intended final recipient of the meteorite in 2105.
Septenio
A 7–8 year cycle of institutional custody. The basic unit of the Time Slot Stewardship Protocol.
Slot
A defined period of custody within the overall 2018–2105 arc. Each slot is a tokenized unit with an assigned institutional holder, on-chain record, and associated rights/obligations.
Slot token
A non-fungible token representing a specific custody period. Minted when a slot period arrives and unlocked to the holder upon successful claim.
$HYPER
The primary governance token of the Hyper Institution. Limited supply, deflationary, used for voting and deliberation rather than speculation.
TSSP
Time Slot Stewardship Protocol. The full operational protocol governing how custody transfers occur, how slots are claimed, and how proof-of-care is documented.
White Paper
The founding document of the Hyper Institution. Current version: v0.1. Code name: HYPER GEOLOGY.
